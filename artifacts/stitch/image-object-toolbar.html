The Future of Urban Architecture

Alex Morgan
• Oct 24, 2023 • 8 min read

Modern architecture has evolved significantly over the last century. From the brutalist movements of the mid-20th century to the sustainable, glass-heavy designs of today, the landscape of our cities is constantly changing. We are no longer just building for shelter; we are building for the environment, for community, and for the future.

![Urban Facade](assets/skyscrapers.jpg)

The interplay of light and structure is what defines this new era. As we move forward, the integration of green spaces within vertical structures becomes not just an aesthetic choice, but a necessity for urban heat regulation.

Consider the "Vertical Forest" concept in Milan. It challenges the traditional concrete jungle narrative, offering a glimpse into a symbiotic relationship between nature and architecture.
